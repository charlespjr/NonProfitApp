# VIDEO 2 — "How to Use Quorum" (Tutorial, 5–7 minutes)

**Purpose:** walk a brand-new user from signup to their first board vote, step by step, so they never need support.
**Audience:** a nonprofit founder who just heard about Quorum; assume zero technical skill.
**Tone:** patient, encouraging instructor — a friend showing you around. Second person ("you click…", "you'll see…"). Celebrate small wins.
**Hard rules:** Quorum organizes and guides — it is NOT a law firm; remind viewers once that documents should be reviewed by their attorney. Say "27 steps across 5 stages."

## Script

**INTRO [0:00–0:20]**
> Welcome to Quorum — the portal that takes your nonprofit from idea to fully compliant 501(c)(3), with your whole board on the record. In the next few minutes you'll create your organization, work the launch checklist, send a document for signatures, invite your board, and run your first vote. Let's go.

*Visual: `01-signin.png` full frame.*

**CHAPTER 1 — Create your foundation [0:20–1:00]**
> Go to quorumsuite dot com and tap "Start free." You'll land on Quorum's front door. Click "Create your foundation." Five boxes: your organization's name, your name, your email, a username you'll sign in with, and a password of at least eight characters. Click "Create organization" — and that's it. No card, no waiting. You're standing in your own private portal, and you're its administrator.

*Visual: `01-signin.png` → zoom on "Create your foundation →" link → `02-register.png`, zoom each field as it's named.*

**CHAPTER 2 — Read your dashboard [1:00–1:40]**
> This is home. The ring in the corner is your launch progress — it fills as you complete steps. The four cards below tell you: documents waiting for signatures, documents fully signed, open tasks, and your next meeting. Below that, your next four checklist steps, recent documents, and upcoming meetings. One glance, and you always know exactly where the launch stands.

*Visual: `03-dashboard.png` — start full, zoom the ring, then across the four KPI cards, then the checklist snapshot.*

**CHAPTER 3 — Work the Launch Checklist [1:40–2:30]**
> Click "Launch Checklist" in the sidebar. Here's the roadmap: twenty-seven steps across five stages — forming the corporation, holding your first board meeting, banking and insurance, tax exemption, and getting ready to raise money. Do them top to bottom: the order matters. EIN before the bank account. Federal exemption before state. Under every step, the gray text tells you exactly where to go, what it costs, and any deadline. When you finish a step, tap its checkbox — the ring on your dashboard moves. Steps with paperwork attached have an "Open" button that jumps straight to the document.

*Visual: `04-checklist.png` — pan the stages; zoom one step's help text long enough to read; zoom a checked-off step with strikethrough; zoom an "Open" button.*

**CHAPTER 4 — Send a document for signatures [2:30–3:20]**
> Now click "Documents." Every governance document your launch needs is already here — bylaws, minutes, resolutions, donor letters — each with a plain-English explanation of what it is and what to do. The badge shows its status: Draft, Awaiting signatures, or Fully signed. Click "Open in DocuSeal" on any document and you'll see the signer roster — your whole board, with a progress bar. "Email board to sign" notifies everyone at once; the bar fills as signatures come in, and you can nudge stragglers with one click. One reminder from us: have your attorney review documents before they're signed — Quorum organizes the work; your counsel blesses it.

*Visual: `05-documents.png` — zoom a description line, then a status badge; `06-docuseal-modal.png` — zoom the roster and progress bar, then the buttons.*

**CHAPTER 5 — Invite your board [3:20–4:10]**
> Head to "Team and Access." Click "Add member." Enter their name, a username, and their personal email — board members don't need a special work email. Click "Generate" for a temporary password, share it with them privately, and set their permissions: Vote lets them weigh in on motions, Sign lets them sign documents. When they first log in, Quorum makes them choose their own private password. This page is also where your subscription lives — pick monthly or yearly and a plan whenever you're ready to upgrade.

*Visual: `13-team-access.png` — zoom the Add member button, then the subscription card with the Monthly/Yearly toggle; `14-manage-access-modal.png` — zoom name/username/email fields, the Generate button, the Vote/Sign toggles.*

**CHAPTER 6 — Run your first board vote [4:10–5:10]**
> Time for a decision. In "Board Votes," click "New motion." Describe what the board is deciding — say, adopting the bylaws — and optionally link it to a meeting. Creating the motion does three things at once: schedules a Zoom discussion, adds it to the calendar, and emails every voting director. Here's exactly what lands in their inbox: the motion, the meeting time, the Zoom link, and one button — "Review and vote in the portal." Each director taps For, Against, or Abstain. The tally bar moves live, and when a majority is reached, the badge flips to Passed. Every decision, on the record, forever.

*Visual: `09-new-motion-modal.png` — zoom the form and the info note; `07-votes.png` — zoom the tally bar and a director row's For/Against/Abstain buttons; `08-email-preview.png` — zoom the subject line and the big button.*

**CHAPTER 7 — Let AI draft the paperwork [5:10–5:45]**
> When a motion passes, don't open a blank document — click "Draft document with AI." Quorum writes the formal board resolution for you: the recitals, the operative clauses, and a signature block for every director. Edit anything you like, have your attorney give it a once-over, then "Send to DocuSeal" routes it to the whole board for signature. Your vote just became its own paperwork.

*Visual: `10-ai-draft-modal.png` — slow pan down the drafted resolution text; zoom "Send to DocuSeal."*

**CHAPTER 8 — Calendar & Notes [5:45–6:15]**
> Two more friends to meet. The Calendar connects to Google Calendar and Zoom once, then every meeting and government deadline lives in one view — one click joins the call. And Notes is your private scratchpad — meeting prep, reminders, follow-ups — autosaved, and visible to no one but you.

*Visual: `11-calendar.png` — zoom a Join Zoom button; `12-notes.png` — brief.*

**OUTRO [6:15–6:30]**
> That's the whole loop: checklist, documents, signatures, votes, meetings — one login, one place. You're ready. Your board is one place now. Quorum — The Nonprofit Board OS.

*Visual: `03-dashboard.png` settle; end card: "app.quorumsuite.com — Start free".*
