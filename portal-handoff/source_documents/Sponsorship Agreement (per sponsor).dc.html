a. Purpose. The Sponsor's contribution supports the Festival and the charitable programs of the Corporation.

b. Use of Name & Logo. The Sponsor grants the Corporation a limited license to use its name and logo solely for recognition in connection with the Festival. The Corporation will use reasonable efforts to provide the recognition described above.

f. Cancellation. If the Festival is cancelled, the Corporation may apply the contribution to its charitable programs or, at the Sponsor's request, to a future event.

Reference

What each sponsorship level means

The higher the level, the more a sponsor supports the festival — and the more visibility they receive in return. Final pricing for each level is set by the Board.

Presenting SponsorTop level · only one

What it means: The headline partner — the festival is presented “in partnership with” them. Only one company holds this spot each year, so it's the most prominent and exclusive.

What they get: Name in the festival title and on everything (banner, website, program, t-shirts), the best booth location, a moment to speak on stage, 10 event passes with VIP hospitality, and their own social posts and a press mention.

Gold Sponsor

What it means: A major supporter — highly visible, just below the presenting partner.

What they get: Prominent logo on the banner, website, and program; a large (10×20) booth; recognition from the stage; 6 event passes; and a dedicated social media feature.

Silver Sponsor

What it means: A solid mid-level supporter — great for local businesses that want real visibility.

What they get: Logo on the website and program, a standard (10×10) booth, 4 event passes, a group social mention, and a quarter-page ad in the program.

Community Sponsor

What it means: An accessible entry level for small businesses, families, and individuals who want to help.

What they get: Their name listed on the website and program, the option to share a table, 2 event passes, and a group social mention.

In-Kind / FriendDonated goods, services, or a gift of any size

What it means: Instead of cash, a sponsor donates products, services, or space the festival would otherwise pay for (food, printing, a stage, a raffle prize).

What they get: Recognition at the level matching the value of what they gave.
